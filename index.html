Women in Graphic Communications

Contributions made by women in the Graphic Communications Industry.

  • Learn more

Introduction

The Graphic Communications Industry is currently one of the largest industries in the world. Over the years as this industry keeps growing so does the contributions made by women. However how did this all start? Who were the first women in this industry and who made it big? This industry did not only help women get careers and fulfill their passion but also helped them voice their concerns about equality through different types of media outlets. This website will take you over the heartful journey of women in the Graphic Communications Industry.

When it All Began

During the 1950s and the 1960s, young women around the world started to get interested in this industry.

Women Employment Rate

  • A 61% increase has been detected in the Graphic Designing field.
  • The rate of being a Creative Director has also increased by 29%.
  • However, currently only 4.8% of women are CEOs in the Graphic Communications industry.

Circumstances Today for Women

  • Though the industry is male-dominated, women are beginning to pursue a degree in this career field.
  • A survey conducted by Clemson University’s Graphic Communication program discovered that:
    • 79% of the students were females
    • while, 21% of the students were male.

Gender Equality in Pay

According research conducted in 2017 by Honeybook’s, women make 32% less than men in the industry.

Influential Women in the Industry

Though this indsutry has been male domniated, there have been some influential women who have paved the way for the future genration. Below are some influential figures that have largely contributed towards the Graphic Communication Industry.

Hilda Dallas

Hilda Dallas is one of the first female designers during the beginning of the 1900s. She was not only an artist, but also a feminist activist who was currently at that time fighting for women rights. By using her passion for justice, she created motivating and informational posters promoting women rights. Thanks to these posters, women slowly started to stand up for themselves and fight for their rights. Today, one of the reasons why women are given the right to work and vote is thanks to the contributions of female designers like Hilda Dallas.

Cipe Pinles

Cipe Pinles was a well-known editor designer from the 1950s to the 1960s. She worked with many magazine companies such as Seventeen, Charm and Mademoiselle. Some of these companies became very well-known and are still growing till this day. Pinles was the first one to start the concept of making magazine designs that were both minimal and artistic at the same time. Many magazine companies to this day, use her concepts to publish their work. Her work remains to be influential for the Graphic Communications Industry.

Muriel Cooper

Muriel Cooper was a graphic designer during the 1950s to the 1960s. She was not only known to be a graphic designer, but also she was well known as a print designer. She made a lot of contributions in both the printing and publishing industries. One of the well-known contributions Cooper made, was creating the logo design for the famous printing and publishing company, MIT Press.

April Greiman

April Greiman was also a graphic designer and a typographer during the 1970s to the 1980s. Greiman was a woman who wanted to create something outside of the box. She was tired of the creating the same boring designs and concepts that were frequentyly being used in the industry. So when the era of technology began to rise, she started using software technology as a form of making art to create a new form of design. From here she created the "New Wave" also known as Swiss Punk Typography. This design concept was very popular as it was new and had a fresh retro look. It soon became a trend and was used in many different types of media outlet. Even to this day, this design concept is seen to be used in many ways such as album covers, posters and much more.

Paula Scher

Paula Scher was a world-renowned graphic designer since the 1970s. She used to be known for being an art director and her exquisite take on typography. After a lot of dedication and hard work in 1991, she became a partner for a well-known design studio called Pentagram. Here, she helped companies develop a brand for themselves, helped with publications and packaging designs. Some of the companies Paula had worked with were Microsoft, Coca-Cola, Tiffany & Co. and Museum of Modern Arts.

Susan Kare

Susan Kare was a digital artist from the beginning of the 1980s. As computer technology was growing, new jobs were created, especially for the Graphic Communications industry. One of the highly sought out jobs was being a specialist designer. Kare worked as a specialist designer for Apple. Here she created many interface elements, designs, fonts, and icons for the launch of the Apple Macintosh computer. One of the biggest reasons why the Macintosh became a huge hit was because of the interface design. She later decided to open her own graphic designing firm where she worked with many famous companies like Microsoft, Facebook, IBM, PayPal and much more. Kare contributed not only to the graphic communications industry but to the whole world as she was one of the first few to introduce the idea and concept of UI/UX design (user-friendly interface).
